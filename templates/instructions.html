General Instructions

Welcome to the ECON 3310 Experiment Platform.

You will participate in 10 rounds of simple decision problems. Each round should take about 1 minute or less. Overall, the whole process should take no more than 15 minutes, including reviewing the instructions.

Before the experiment begins, we randomly pick an integer number between 1 and 10 and denote it by R. This number is kept secret until the end of the experiment. The points you earn in this experiment will depend on your WEALTH in round R.

In each round, you start with an endowment of 100 points. You will then choose how much of this endowment (x) to invest in a risky asset and how much to keep. You have 1 minute to decide. There is no right or wrong answer: choose a number that reflects your comfort with risk.

At each round, your remaining endowment (100 − x) is kept. Your investment x generates a random return determined by a fair coin flip: if heads, your investment becomes 2.5 × x; if tails, it becomes 0. Therefore, your WEALTH in that round will be 100 − x + 2.5x with probability ½ (heads) and 100 − x with probability ½ (tails).

Returns are independent across rounds.

At the end of the 10th round, we reveal R. The points you earn in the experiment are your WEALTH in round R.
